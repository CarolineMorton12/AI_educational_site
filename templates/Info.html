How to build an ANN

Click on the arrows to learn how ANNs work

According to Josh McCarthy, " It is the science and engineering of making intelligent machines, especially intelligent computer programs. It is related to the similar task of using computers to understand human intelligence, but AI does not have to confine itself to methods that are biologically observable." This means that machines can "think" in their own way, and this could be helpful for us!

There are lots of different types of AI (see here if you want to learn more), but here we will just be focusing on Machine learning, specifically classification. Machines are very good at pattern recognition! They can often see links that humans cannot - this is why they are often used to sort things. This is also the reason they are used for neural networks!

Neural networks learn to do something by looking for patterns in data. For example, there could be a NN that was learning to say if a picture was of a cat or dog. It would be fed a lot of data like this:

It would then find patterns in the data (even experts don't know how they do it!) and eventually would be able to recognise between a cat and a dog!

pro tip : The accuracy of the NN depends greatly on the data that it is being trained on. If the data is diverse the NN will be able to get a better sense of what actually it is doing, but usually this is difficult as collecting datasets is very time consuming!
